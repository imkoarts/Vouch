# Standalone X-Post Contract

Use for `X_POST` and short original posts that are not replies or quote posts.

## Goal

Write a compact post with one reason to exist.

The post may contain analysis, but it should read like a direct observation, not a compressed strategy memo, product brief, research abstract, or thought-leadership template.

## Default output

- one or two sentences
- one controlling observation
- one supporting condition, consequence, mechanism, or example at most
- ordinary precise verbs
- concrete actors and objects
- no mandatory hook
- no mandatory conclusion
- no rhetorical reveal

Three sentences are allowed only when each contributes separate information.

## Literal-first process

Before styling, write the idea in literal form:

1. Who or what acts?
2. What happens?
3. Under what condition?
4. What concrete object must the reader understand?

Then remove any sentence that merely announces, reframes, or elevates the literal point.

## Analytical-packaging failures

### Abstract test framing

Do not describe an event mainly as a “test” when the sentence can state what the event will reveal.

Bad:

> The tournament gives the format a narrow test.

Better:

> The tournament should show whether traders can read combo markets once volume picks up.

“Test” is valid when it refers to a defined test with actual criteria.

### Staged thesis

Do not arrange the post as:

1. setup
2. “the practical question”
3. reveal

Bad:

> The practical question is not only whether more categories can be listed. It is whether the combination remains legible.

Better:

> Adding categories will not help if traders cannot tell what each price includes.

Use only when the relation is supported.

### Manufactured `not only X, but Y`

Flag semantic equivalents of:

- not only X; the real issue is Y
- less about X, more about Y
- X is one thing; Y is what matters
- the question is not X; it is Y

when the contrast mainly manufactures depth.

Contrast is allowed when both sides are necessary and supported.

### Abstract noun density

Flag a sentence when meaning is carried mainly by conceptual nouns and weak predicates.

These words are not banned:

- format
- test
- question
- combination
- legibility
- expansion
- adoption
- experience
- implication
- behavior
- utility

Revise when several abstractions interact and the reader cannot quickly picture the action.

### Generic user proxy

Avoid vague `people`, `users`, or `the market` when the source supports a more precise actor such as traders, buyers, developers, voters, customers, or tournament participants.

Do not invent a narrower actor when the evidence does not support one.

### Product-memo voice

Flag detached product-strategy phrasing that does not fit an ordinary post.

The issue is the communicative function, not any one term.

### Mini-essay arc

A short post should not automatically contain:

- contextual opening
- analytical reframing
- thesis reveal
- implication
- polished ending

If the first sentences merely prepare the final sentence, state the final point directly.

## Sentence-job test

Each sentence must add one unique job:

- observation
- fact
- condition
- mechanism
- consequence
- example
- caveat
- useful question

Reject jobs such as:

- setup for the insight
- announcing the practical question
- making the idea sound important
- transitioning toward the conclusion
- completing the mini-essay

## Output test

The post passes when:

- its main point can be expressed literally
- the final version does not inflate that point into a reveal
- readers can identify the actor, action, condition, and object
- every sentence adds information
- the wording fits X rather than a product memo
- deleting the final sentence would remove real content
