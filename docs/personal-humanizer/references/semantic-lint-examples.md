# Semantic Lint Examples

These examples demonstrate functions, not phrase replacements.

## 1. Announcement before content

Before:

> The most interesting part is that residential IPs do not prove organic behavior.

After:

> Residential IPs do not prove the behavior was organic.

The first version advertises the point before making it.

## 2. Importance announcement

Before:

> The uncomfortable truth is that the export may still depend on one provider.

After, when supported:

> The exported evals still use the provider’s schema.

The second version names the limitation instead of announcing severity.

## 3. False agency

Before:

> The market rewarded the company for cutting features.

After, when supported:

> Customers kept renewing after the company cut the features.

The revision names the observed behavior rather than assigning intention to “the market.”

## 4. Accurate inanimate subject

Keep:

> the API returned the same error twice

The API is the literal technical subject. No hidden human actor is required.

## 5. Passive voice when the actor is unknown

Keep:

> three accounts were suspended overnight

Do not invent who suspended them when the source does not say.

## 6. Narrator distance

Before:

> People always believe domestic traffic is organic.

After, when the source supports only one case:

> The post treats domestic network origin and organic activity as separate findings.

The revision narrows the scope.

## 7. Do not force “you”

Before:

> People tend to accept the default.

Bad mechanical repair:

> You tend to accept the default.

Better, when supported:

> Most participants kept the default setting.

## 8. Unasked reassurance

Before:

> The first draft may be rough, and that’s okay.

After:

> The first draft may be rough.

In an editing instruction, the reassurance adds no task value.

## 9. Permission-granting ending

Before:

> You can leave the conclusion unresolved. Not every post needs a lesson, and that’s okay.

After:

> You can leave the conclusion unresolved.

The permission ending repeats the instruction emotionally.

## 10. Sentence without a job

Before:

> The clause covers training. This distinction matters. It does not define export rights.

After:

> The clause covers training, not export rights.

“This distinction matters” contributes no separate information.

## 11. Explanation after punchline

Before:

> finally an official anti-yap switch. this is funny because models often produce answers that are too long

After:

> finally an official anti-yap switch

The punchline already carries the observation.

## 12. Metronomic rhythm

Before:

> The provider stores the traces. The provider scores the evals. The provider controls the export.

After, when all claims are supported:

> The provider stores the traces, scores the evals, and controls the export.

Do not force this repair when separate sentences create intentional timing.

## 13. Mechanical triad

Before:

> The reply should be direct, concise, and clear.

After:

> The reply should be direct.

Keep all three only when they identify genuinely separate requirements.

## 14. Useful three-item list

Keep:

> The export contains the test case, expected answer, and score.

All three items carry distinct factual content.

## 15. Useful question

Keep:

> which accounts were included in the sample?

The question requests missing information. It is not engagement bait.

## 16. Useful Wh-opening

Keep:

> what changed after the algorithm update?

The opening is the natural form of the requested question.

## 17. Useful adverb

Keep:

> the claim was independently verified

“Independently” changes the evidentiary meaning.

## 18. Useful em dash

Keep when it fits the voice:

> domestic IP, coordinated behavior — both can be true

Do not add or remove punctuation mechanically.

## 19. Micro reply: announcement removed

Before:

> the interesting part is that no vpn does not mean no coordination

After:

> no vpn still doesn’t mean no coordination

## 20. Micro reply: narrator distance removed

Before:

> people always confuse where traffic came from with who was behind it

After:

> a residential ip shows the connection, not who ran the account

Use only when that distinction is supported by the source or supplied evidence.
